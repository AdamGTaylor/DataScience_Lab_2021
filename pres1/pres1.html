Presentation 1. - Lightning Hotspots on Earth¶

This presentation is about showing progress, succesful or not. On this thought, let's see first what failed:

Localising Workflow¶

Due to unaccessibility to kooplex, I wanted to localise my workflow. This meant that I had to obtain data for myself, process it locally, and output the simplfied file, which will be used in the future to produce the heatmap like graphs.

Obtaning Data¶

The acquisition of data turned out to be rather easy - only that the site asked for a lot of information about the user. After that, is authentication must be used to download uncompressed data, that is in netCDF4 of HDF4 format. For the downloading, the site gives a linklist in a text that is utilized by wget. As I wanted to localize my workflow on a Windows based device, I had to download wget.

Turns out that one of the flags is not good on for wget, thus I had to move to a linux based distribution. Ubuntus now come with wget, which meant that I could easy download the data - which took around 1.5 hours.

Fileformat Issues¶

HDF5s are easily usable, as shown previously, h5py can easily read these, but I had HDF4s which are not in the same boat. It turned out that I had to do a conversion, which is not that reliable and works on linux only. At this point, I surrendered the thought of localised workflow while simultaneously kooplex becoming avaliable.
